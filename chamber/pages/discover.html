Discover Barquisimeto

History

The history of Barquisimeto as a city begins in 1552 when the Segovian officer Juan de Villegas was in El Tocuyo, the first city founded in western Venezuela, the center of operations from where the process of the conquest began.

In 1556, the town began to be built again, this time in a place called El Carabalí. This time under the name of Nueva Segovia de Barquisimeto. When the small city began to solidify, it was turned into ashes by the tyrant Lope de Aguirre in 1561. This action caused the small city to change its settlement for the third time. On that occasion Nueva Segovia managed to settle in the place known as "Desembocadero de Barquisimeto" where the Turbio and Claro rivers converge. Unfortunately, it suffered heavy flooding due to the instability of the chosen site, so it moved again.

Finally, and for the fourth time, Nueva Segovia de Barquisimeto changed location in 1563, occupying the high plateau where it is currently located, eleven years after Juan de Villegas founded Nueva Segovia de Buría. It is from this settlement that the city truly began to develop, whose most outstanding characteristics were the mathematical symmetry with which it was built in an advanced population in terms of its colonial aspect, whose development from that very moment was uninterrupted and growing, finding us with the current Barquisimeto.

Geography

Barquisimeto is located on the terrace of the same name, on the banks of the ”Río Turbio”. Barquisimeto is located in the center-west of Venezuela (353 kilometers (219 mi) from Caracas, the capital of the country), which can be traveled in 4 to 5 hours by car.

Demographics

Barquisimeto has an estimated population of 1,972,233 inhabitants and a territory of 81,700 square kilometers, which represents 8.9% of the national surface and 16.5% of the population.

Economy

Barquisimeto's location in the interior of Venezuela, allows it to be a confluence of land routes between the central and western regions, with the western plains and the south, with the coastal axis to the north, with the Andes to the west and with Zulia to the northwest. In addition, it is located next to a fertile valley, which has contributed to agricultural production, the development of commerce, service and manufacturing industries, which are the main economic activities of the urban area. Being located on a high plateau, the city of Barquisimeto is one of the few Venezuelan cities with a hypodamic urban plan.

After a deep stagnation in the nineties, in this decade, there has been a huge rebound in terms of investment in construction, industry, commerce and services, which has boosted the growth of this city, which has consolidated its position as the fourth largest city in Venezuela. This is why in the last three years, numerous shopping malls, hotels for all levels, office buildings, residences and business centers have appeared in all its growth poles.

We can also highlight the proximity to the twin cities of Acarigua and Araure, the Agroindustrial and Food Pole Center of the country, which facilitates trade relations and the development of the city's food activity, thus having a great economic growth between the two cities.

In this city, urban activities linked to industry, commerce and services stand out, as well as the exploitation and transformation of non-metallic minerals and agricultural production, mainly coffee harvested in the Rio Claro area.

In addition, it concentrates more than 80 percent of the State's manufacturing establishments, with a strong orientation towards food production and the manufacture of foundry and metal mechanical products.

Since 1983, the largest wholesale market in Venezuela, known as MERCABAR (Mercado Mayorista de Alimentos de Barquisimeto or Wholesale Food Market of Barquisimeto), has existed in Barquisimeto's Industrial Zone III, with a land area of 108 hectares (42 of which are developed), whose main activity is the production of public services for sale and whose income comes from this activity. One of its main objectives is to provide, maintain and manage adequate spaces for the organization of wholesale trade in its area of influence.

Weather

In Barquisimeto, the warm climate is dominant although it is attenuated due to its altitude at about 650 meters (0.40 mi) above sea level, which makes its climate pleasant to what it should normally be. In Barquisimeto, summers are short, very hot, muggy, dry and cloudy and winters are short, hot, oppressive, wet and mostly cloudy. During the course of the year, the temperature generally varies from 20 °C (68 ºF) to 31 °C (87.8 ºF) and rarely drops below 19 °C (66.2 ºF) or rises above 34 °C (93.2 ºF).

Tourism

On the occasion of its quadricentennial in 1952, the highest obelisk in Venezuela was erected in Barquisimeto, from where you can contemplate its famous sunsets, which have made it called the "City of twilight". Apart from Barquisimeto, which has cultural and musical activities, museums, temples and theaters, it is worth visiting the historic and colonial area of Carora, the relics of El Tocuyo. Quibor with its museums and famous handicrafts and archeology; Sanare, a beautiful town nestled in the Andes, where you can also buy beautiful handicrafts. We can not fail to recommend a visit to Humocaro, Alto and Bajo, the latter with a beautiful Bolivar Square, at the foot of a high mountain and surrounded by beautiful chaguaramos.

Culture

Barquisimeto is a place for multitudinous celebrations, such as: The International Carnivals of Barquisimeto (from February toMarch), where you can observe a great variety of floats, and musical artistic shows of recognized trajectory, The International Fair of Barquisimeto which begins on September 14 (anniversary of the foundation of Barquisimeto), and culminates on September 25 of each year, where you can appreciate, among many things, diverse exhibitions and musical shows, and finally, a varied range of options to enjoy Christmas in Barquisimeto, where you can observe diverse exhibitions, musical shows and many fireworks. It is customary to welcome the New Year in the Flor de Venezuela, with family and friends.

It has numerous cultural centers. Some of the most important are the Ateneo de Barquisimeto, the Juarez Theater, the "Pio Tamayo" Public Library, the "Pascual Venegas Filardo" Public Library, the Oliver Soto Chess House Pascual Venegas Filardo" Public Library, the Oliver Soto Chess House, the Eustoquio Gómez Culture House, the Guachirongo Popular Culture Center, the General Jacinto Lara Military Archeological Museum, the La Salle Museum, the Barquisimeto Museum, the Divina Pastora Museum, the Charles Chaplin Cinema Club, and the "Juan Carmona" Exhibition Hall (headquarters of the newspaper "El Impulso").
